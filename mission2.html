Chess For Beginners

Introduction

Chess is a board game played by 2 players. Each player takes turns moving a piece of their colour (usually black or white), with the final objective of the game being to "Checkmate" the opposing player. Each player starts with a total of 16 pieces consisting of 6 different types. There are:

  • 8 Pawns
  • 2 Rooks (Also known as Castles)
  • 2 Knights
  • 2 Bishops
  • 1 Queen
  • 1 King

Board

The chessboard is an 8 x 8 grid of alternating black and white squares. The person playing 'White' will have their pieces arranged in the following order from left to right:

Rook, Knight, Bishop, Queen, King, Bishop, Knight, Rook

The second row, will be lined up with 8 pawns. 'Black's Pieces should mirror the positions of the white pieces.

Pawn

Each player starts with 8 pawns. The pawn can only move one step forward, except for on it's first move when it has the option of moving 2 steps forward. However, When attacking, it can only attack pieces that are diagonally infront (either left or right), and cannot attack pieces directly infront of it.

Check, Checkmate & Stalemate

When the King is in a position such that it can be taken by the opponent in their next turn, it is said to be in Check. In this situation, the payer in Check must move their King out of danger, or place another piece to block the attack.

When the King is in Check and there is no way to get the King out of Check (King cannot move into a space where it is not under attack, and there are no other pieces that can block the attack) , that is considered Checkmate, and that player loses.

Stalemate is when the King is not in Check, and the player has no move where the King is not placed in check, the game is considered in a Stalemate and ends in a draw.
